## üé¨ Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery Call (15-30 min)

**Goal:** Understand pain points and needs

**Questions:**
- What's your biggest customer support challenge?
- How many support requests do you get?
- What languages do you support?
- How much do you spend on support?
- What's your current support team size?
- What tools do you use?

**Outcome:** Qualify or disqualify

### Stage 2: Demo (30-45 min)

**Goal:** Show value with their actual use case

**Agenda:**
1. Show web chatbot (5 min)
2. Upload their FAQs/knowledge base (10 min)
3. Test with real questions (10 min)
4. Show phone support (if they need it) (5 min)
5. Show analytics/cost monitoring & call dashboard (5 min)
6. Discuss pricing and next steps (10 min)

**Preparation:**
- Ask for their FAQs/knowledge base beforehand
- Prepare demo with their content
- Have pricing ready
- Prepare to answer objections

**Outcome:** Pilot commitment or "not right now"

### Stage 3: Pilot

**Goal:** Prove value with real usage

**Setup:**
- Deploy web chatbot for them (or provide access to demo instance)
- Upload their knowledge base
- Set up phone number (if they need phone support - already built)
- Train their team
- Regular check-ins

**Success Criteria:**
- 100+ conversations processed
- 80%+ answer accuracy
- Positive feedback from team
- Cost savings demonstrated

**Outcome:** Convert to paid or extend pilot

### Stage 4: Conversion

**Goal:** Convert pilot to paying customer

**Tactics:**
- Show ROI (cost savings, time saved)
- Address any concerns
- Offer discount for annual commitment
- Make it easy to say yes

**Outcome:** Signed contract or lost deal

## üõ°Ô∏è Handling Objections

### Objection 1: "We already have a support team"

**Response:** "Great! Zoid can handle the repetitive questions (80% of support), freeing your team for complex issues. This actually makes your team more valuable."

**Proof:** Show case study or ROI calculator

### Objection 2: "AI isn't accurate enough"

**Response:** "That's why we use RAG - it only answers from YOUR knowledge base. If it doesn't know, it says so and escalates to humans."

**Proof:** Demo with their actual FAQs

### Objection 3: "Too expensive"

**Response:** "What are you currently spending on support? [Calculate ROI] At $299/month, if you handle 2,000 conversations, that's $0.15 per conversation vs $5-10 for a human agent."

**Proof:** ROI calculator

### Objection 4: "We need phone support"

**Response:** "Great news - we already have phone support built! We can set up a phone number for you. Let's start with web chat to prove value, then we can add phone support for your customers."

**Proof:** Show working phone number (+1 (510) 370 5981) and demo call capability

### Objection 5: "We're not ready yet"

**Response:** "What would need to change for you to be ready? [Listen] Can we check back in [timeframe]?"

**Follow-up:** Add to nurture sequence, check in regularly

## üí° Key Principles

1. **Listen more than you talk** - Understand pain before pitching
2. **Show, don't tell** - Demo with their actual content
3. **Start small** - Pilot ‚Üí Paid ‚Üí Scale
4. **Follow up** - Most sales happen after 5+ touches
5. **Ask for referrals** - Best customers come from referrals
6. **Talk to customers regularly** - Regular customer calls
7. **Ship fast, learn fast** - Don't spend too long on a feature
8. **Revenue is the North Star** - Regularly ask: "Did we get closer to revenue?"
9. **Say No to Feature Creep** - Default answer: "Let's wait"
10. **Celebrate Small Wins** - Momentum matters

## üö¶ Decision Framework

**Before building ANY feature, ask:**

1. **Do customers explicitly ask for it?** (Not "would be nice")
2. **Is it blocking revenue?** (Can't close sales without it)
3. **Can we charge more for it?** (Increases LTV)
4. **Is there a faster way?** (API/service vs building)

**If 3+ answers are NO ‚Üí Skip or defer**
